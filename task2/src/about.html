About Organic Farming

Organic farming is a sustainable agricultural practice that prioritizes environmental stewardship and natural processes. It avoids synthetic chemicals and promotes soil health, biodiversity, and ecosystem balance. By utilizing methods like crop rotation, composting, and biological pest control, organic farmers produce nutritious food while minimizing environmental impact.

Organic farming contributes to healthier ecosystems, reduces pollution, and supports biodiversity. It also offers consumers food products free from synthetic pesticides and genetically modified organisms (GMOs), often resulting in better taste and nutritional quality.
