Broccoli

How to grow :

  1. 1. Choose Varieties: Select broccoli varieties suited for your climate, such as Calabrese or Romanesco.
  2. 2. Prepare Soil: Broccoli thrives in well-drained, fertile soil with a pH of 6.0-7.0. Amend soil with compost before planting.
  3. 3. Planting: Start seeds indoors 6-8 weeks before the last frost or directly sow seeds in early spring or fall. Plant seedlings 18-24 inches apart in rows spaced 24-36 inches apart.
  4. 4. Care: Keep the soil consistently moist and provide ample sunlight (at least 6 hours of full sun daily). Mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
  5. 5. Fertilize: Side-dress with compost or a balanced organic fertilizer when plants are about 4-6 inches tall, and again when they begin to form heads.
  6. 6. Harvest: Harvest broccoli heads when they are firm, tight, and deep green. Cut the main head at an angle, leaving the plant intact to produce side shoots for continued harvesting.

Health Benefits :

Broccoli is a nutritional powerhouse, rich in vitamins C, K, and A, as well as folate, fiber, and antioxidants. It supports immune function, promotes heart health, aids digestion, and may have anti-cancer properties.
