Bell Peppers

How to grow :

  1. 1. Choose Varieties: Select bell pepper varieties suited for your climate, such as California Wonder or King of the North.
  2. 2. Start Seeds: Start seeds indoors 8-10 weeks before the last frost, using seedling trays or pots filled with seed starting mix.
  3. 3. Transplant: Transplant seedlings outdoors after the last frost, spacing them 18-24 inches apart in rows or in raised beds.
  4. 4. Sunlight: Provide full sun exposure for at least 6-8 hours per day. Bell peppers thrive in warm, sunny conditions.
  5. 5. Watering: Water consistently, keeping the soil evenly moist but not waterlogged. Mulch around plants to retain soil moisture.
  6. 6. Fertilize: Fertilize with a balanced organic fertilizer every 4-6 weeks to promote healthy growth and fruit production.
  7. 7. Support: Stake or cage tall varieties to support the weight of the plants and prevent them from bending or breaking.
  8. 8. Harvest: Harvest bell peppers when they reach their mature size, typically 60-90 days after transplanting. Cut the peppers from the plant using sharp scissors or pruning shears.

Health Benefits :

Bell peppers are rich in vitamins C, A, and K, as well as antioxidants. They support immune function, promote eye health, aid digestion, and may reduce the risk of chronic diseases.
