Tomatoes

How to grow :

  1. 1. Choose Varieties: Select tomato varieties suited for your climate and garden space, such as heirloom, cherry, or beefsteak tomatoes.
  2. 2. Start Seeds: Begin seeds indoors 6-8 weeks before the last frost, using seedling trays or pots filled with seed starting mix.
  3. 3. Transplant: Transplant seedlings outdoors after the last frost, spacing them 18-36 inches apart in rows or in large containers.
  4. 4. Support: Provide support for indeterminate varieties with stakes, cages, or trellises to keep plants upright and promote air circulation.
  5. 5. Watering: Water deeply, aiming for 1-2 inches per week, keeping the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged.
  6. 6. Fertilize: Fertilize regularly with balanced organic fertilizer or compost tea, following package instructions.
  7. 7. Pruning: Remove suckers (the growth between the main stem and branches) for indeterminate varieties to focus energy on fruit production.
  8. 8. Harvest: Harvest tomatoes when they are firm, fully colored, and have a glossy appearance. Gently twist or cut the stem to remove fruits.

Health Benefits :

Tomatoes are rich in vitamins A, C, and K, as well as antioxidants like lycopene. They promote heart health, support skin health, aid digestion, and may reduce the risk of certain cancers.
