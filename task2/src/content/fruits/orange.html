Oranges

How to grow :

  1. 1. Choose Varieties: Select orange varieties suited for your climate and space, such as Valencia, Navel, or Blood oranges.
  2. 2. Climate: Oranges thrive in subtropical to tropical climates with warm temperatures and plenty of sunlight.
  3. 3. Soil: Plant oranges in well-drained soil with a pH of 6-7. They prefer sandy loam soil rich in organic matter.
  4. 4. Planting: Plant orange trees in a sunny location protected from frost and strong winds, with enough space for the tree to grow (15-25 feet apart).
  5. 5. Watering: Water newly planted trees deeply and regularly to establish roots. Once established, water oranges deeply every 1-2 weeks during dry periods.
  6. 6. Pruning: Prune orange trees annually during dormancy to remove dead or crowded branches and to shape the tree for optimal growth and fruit production.
  7. 7. Fertilizing: Fertilize orange trees with a balanced organic fertilizer in early spring and midsummer, following package instructions.
  8. 8. Harvest: Harvest oranges when they are fully ripe and have reached their desired color. They are ready to pick when they come off the tree easily with a gentle twist.

Health Benefits :

Oranges are rich in vitamin C, fiber, and antioxidants. They promote immune function, aid digestion, support heart health, and may reduce the risk of chronic diseases.
